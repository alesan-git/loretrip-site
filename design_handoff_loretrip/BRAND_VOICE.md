# LoreTrip — Voice & Tone

The LoreTrip voice is the most distinctive part of the brand. Read this before writing **any** copy — marketing, product, docs, tweets, error messages, button labels, all of it.

---

## The voice in one line

**The quiet expert in the back of the room.**

LoreTrip is confident without being loud. Specific without being boastful. Comfortable letting the work speak. We assume our readers — agency owners — are smart, busy, and skeptical of anyone selling them software. We respect that.

---

## Tonal coordinates

| | |
|---|---|
| **Confident, not cocky** | We know what we're doing. We don't need to remind people. |
| **Specific, not vague** | "Trip planning, branded as yours" beats "the future of travel". |
| **Plainspoken, not corporate** | "We" not "our team". Active voice. Short clauses. |
| **Restrained, not stiff** | Allow dry wit and small understatement. Never sarcasm. |
| **Warm-adjacent, not chummy** | We sound like a colleague, not a friend. |

---

## Three hard rules

These are not preferences. They are inviolable.

### 1. No exclamation marks. Ever.
Not in marketing. Not in product copy. Not in tweets. Not even in the email subject line. The exclamation mark is the universal signal of trying too hard. Confidence doesn't shout.

**Wrong:** "Welcome to LoreTrip! Let's plan something amazing!"
**Right:** "Welcome. Let's get your first trip drafted."

### 2. No emoji in marketing or product copy.
Emoji are loud. They reset the register from "infrastructure" to "consumer app" and we are not a consumer app. Acceptable in informal team comms only.

### 3. The slop list — never use these words

These are the words that make travel marketing copy indistinguishable. If you find yourself reaching for one, the sentence isn't done yet.

| Don't | Why | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| unlock | meaningless verb-as-marketing | enable, give access to, make possible |
| seamless | nobody knows what this means | works without setup, no handoff, runs continuously |
| revolutionary | overpromises | new, different, rebuilt |
| journey (as metaphor) | no | trip, plan, itinerary |
| adventure | this is a B2B platform | trip, route, itinerary |
| magic / magical | hand-wave | (describe what it actually does) |
| transform | filler verb | rewrite, replace, redo |
| effortless | usually a lie | quick, low-effort, automatic |
| wanderlust | belongs in 2014 | (cut the sentence) |
| powered by AI | meaningless in 2026 | (describe what the model does specifically) |
| supercharge | startup deck filler | speed up, cut from X to Y |
| journey of discovery | retire | (cut) |
| your customers will love | nobody believes this | (describe what changes for them concretely) |

## Audience

**Always:** small-to-mid travel agency owners and operators.

**Never:** travelers, end-clients of agencies, or "the trip-planning consumer."

This matters because every piece of copy we write should make sense when read by a 47-year-old agency owner who has been booking small-group sailing trips in the Adriatic for fifteen years and is rightfully suspicious of new software. If a sentence wouldn't survive that reader, cut it.

---

## Sentence-level rules

- **Short sentences default.** Aim for under 18 words. Long sentences must earn the length.
- **Active voice.** "We built this for X." Not "This was built for X."
- **Specific over abstract.** "12-day itineraries with 80+ partner ferries" beats "comprehensive logistics."
- **Cut adverbs.** "Truly", "really", "incredibly" → delete. The sentence is fine without.
- **No throat-clearing.** Don't open with "We're excited to announce" or "We believe that." Just say the thing.

## Sample voice — passing & failing

### Marketing hero

❌ **Failing**
> Unlock the magic of seamless trip planning. Powered by AI, LoreTrip transforms your agency into a modern travel powerhouse — and your clients will love every effortless moment of their journey.

(Hits five slop words, two exclamation-energy phrases, talks to travelers instead of agencies, says nothing.)

✅ **Passing**
> LoreTrip is the AI trip-planning platform behind specialist travel agencies. We do the software. You stay the brand.

### Feature description

❌ **Failing**
> Our revolutionary itinerary engine empowers your team to craft truly personalized journeys at lightning speed.

✅ **Passing**
> Build a 12-day itinerary in under five minutes. Pull from your own supplier network — not ours.

### Error state

❌ **Failing**
> Oops! Something went wrong. Don't worry, we're on it.

✅ **Passing**
> Couldn't reach the supplier database. Retry, or continue offline — drafts will sync when the connection returns.

### Partner badge

❌ **Failing**
> Magically powered by LoreTrip ✨

✅ **Passing**
> Powered by LoreTrip

### Confirmation

❌ **Failing**
> Awesome! Your trip has been saved!

✅ **Passing**
> Saved.

---

## When to break the rules

Almost never. The few exceptions:

- **Plain humor** is allowed in places where personality matters (a 404 page, an out-of-office auto-reply). Still no exclamation marks.
- **Long-form pieces** (case studies, thought-leadership) can ease the short-sentence default. The voice should still be recognizable.
- **Direct quotes from agency owners** in case studies should not be edited to fit our voice. Let them sound like themselves.

---

## Quick checklist before publishing any copy

- [ ] Zero exclamation marks
- [ ] Zero emoji
- [ ] Zero words from the slop list
- [ ] Zero throat-clearing openers ("We're excited to…")
- [ ] Speaks to agency owners, not travelers
- [ ] Specific (numbers, names, concrete verbs) wherever possible
- [ ] No sentence over 25 words without good reason
- [ ] Reads cleanly aloud
